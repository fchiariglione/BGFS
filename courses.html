Overview of BGFS Courses

Our programs foster the development of twenty-first-century STEAM skills in multimedia authorship, critical thinking, ethnic studies, digital literacy, and technological decision-making. The BGFS foundations curriculum places the Black woman in film subject matter expert at the center of the experience or exchange, and then we go from there. A curriculum of key production related courses designed to give history, craft and technical foundations of motion picture processes and best practices.

COURSE LISTINGS
Black Girl Film School Foundations I

In this course, students will examine a variety of creative and technical production roles by learning about the contributions of Black women to the American Media industry. These roles include: screenwriters, directors, cinematographers, editors, and costume designers.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: fall duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm

Black Girl Film School Foundations II

In this course, students will build upon their knowledge from BGFS Film Foundations I and apply what they’ve learned about the different creative and technical production roles to a 10-week project. By the end of the course, students will walk away with a Director’s notebook they can use to produce a branded new media project.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: Spring and Summer duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm

Black Girl Film School: History of the Pioneers

In this course, students will learn how to be active viewers of film and media while learning about transformative BGFS Pioneer filmmakers. We will watch, analyze, discuss, apply and evaluate work of Black female film pioneers across form and genre. Throughout the course we will explore the history and craft of five different Black women in film pioneers and document best practices from their work in our own Pioneer reference workbooks.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: fall duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm

Specialties

BGFS Screenwriting Foundations I: Process & formatting

In this course, students will learn the technical rules and best practices to draft a professionally formatted screenplay using industry standard screenwriting software, writing development templates and editing tools.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: spring duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm
BGFS Screenwriting Foundations I ONLINE COURSE
Admissions applications are accepted on a rolling basis.
BGFS Directing Foundations I

In this course, students will learn the “jobs” that a Director has to do before they step one foot onto a production set to direct their film. This course will build upon the Director’s notebook created in BGFS Foundations 1 with the development of a scene analysis, shot lists and floor plans.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: spring duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm
BGFS Directing Foundations I ONLINE COURSE
Admissions applications are accepted on a rolling basis.
BGFS Cinematography Foundations I

In this course, students will learn over 25 different types of composition techniques and when to use them to craft strong visual language behind the camera. Students will build upon the Director’s notebook created in BGFS Foundations II with a specific focus on cinematography and the relationship between the DP and the Director.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: spring duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm
BGFS Cinematography Foundations I ONLINE COURSE
Admissions applications are accepted on a rolling basis.
BGFS Camera Foundations I

In this course, students will dive into a high level introduction to camera use, filmmaking and editing designed to offer opportunities to explore both technical and artistic choices behind the camera. A mix of assigned media, writing assignments, reading, planning, and hands-on media making will guide students while learning the basics of the digital media production process.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: Summer and Fall duration: 10 weeks
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week Times of live instructions: 1 hour w/ instructor and learning Coach Weds/Thursday at 4pm
BGFS Camera Foundations I ONLINE COURSE
Admissions applications are accepted on a rolling basis.
BGFS Editing Foundations I

In this course, students will learn the technical rules and best practices to edit a 30 second segment using industry standard editing software, post production templates and editing tools.

COURSE LOGISTICS Time Offered: Fall duration: 10 weeks Application Period: dates
COURSE SCHEDULE Self-guided, 4 hours of course work per week
BGFS Editing Foundations I ONLINE COURSE

BGFS Costume Design Foundations I

In this course students will learn the “jobs” that a Costume Designer has to do before they step one foot onto a production set to shoot a film. This course will lay the foundations for a Costume design project by bringing a single character to life through the best practices of Costume Design research and planning.
